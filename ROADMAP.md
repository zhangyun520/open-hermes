# Roadmap / 路线图

Jellyfish evolves through a **wide entrance, narrow trunk** process: many people can propose directions, while reviewed work moves through a small accountable trunk.

## Phase 1: Concept Stabilization

- Clarify vocabulary, values, safety boundaries, and contribution types.
- Collect ideas, failure cases, and benchmark scenarios.
- Keep raw private data out of public artifacts.

## Phase 2: MVP

- Keep core flows deterministic and mock-first.
- Implement residual cards, review gates, crystals, coding organs, and local tests.
- Record important contributions in `CONTRIBUTORS.md`.

## Phase 3: Community Co-Creation

- Enable GitHub Issues, Discussions, Projects, and RFC review loops.
- Invite contributors to submit ideas, failures, benchmarks, code, docs, research notes, and product designs.
- Promote only reviewed and redacted structures into shared commons.

## Phase 4: Research Release

- Publish reproducible benchmark packs and protocol notes.
- Separate public eval samples from private raw data.
- Document limits, failures, and open questions.

## Phase 5: Ecosystem

- Build safe integration pathways with explicit consent and human review.
- Support community-maintained modules without letting unreviewed work become canonical.
- Keep governance accountable as the contributor base grows.
